TRAP SENSITIVITY PROTOCOL

If upper traps feel tight/irritated (>5/10 discomfort):
Immediate: Stop all pulling exercises. Replace with Cable Chest Fly (3 x 15) + Incline DB Press (2 x 12). Extra PT: Prone Y-Raise (4 x 15), Levator Scap Stretch (2 min each).
Next 48 hours: Heat + gentle stretching 2-3x daily. Chin tucks: 3 x 10, every 2-3 hours. NO pulling movements.
Next workout: Reduce all pulling loads by 50%. If still uncomfortable, skip pulling entirely for 1 week.
Return to normal when: Trap tension <3/10, can depress shoulders without discomfort, no pain with cable rows at 50% load.

EXERCISE TIPS & FORM GUIDES

Click exercise names above to jump here. Watch videos before your first session.

Dead Bugs

Key cues: Press lower back into floor (no gap). Move opposite arm/leg slowly. Exhale as you extend.

  • If back arches, reduce range of motion
  • Quality > speed - 3 sec out, 3 sec back
YouTube: Squat University - Dead Bug

Bird Dogs

Key cues: Keep hips level (don't rotate). Reach long, not high. Brace core throughout.

  • Imagine balancing a cup of water on your lower back
  • Stop if hips drop or rotate
YouTube: Squat University - Bird Dog

Side Plank

Key cues: Stack shoulders over elbow. Hips up high. Straight line from head to heels.

  • Don't let hips sag or pike up
  • Modified version: bend bottom knee
YouTube: Squat University - Side Plank

Clamshells (Banded)

Key cues: Keep feet together, rotate from hip. Don't let pelvis roll back. Feel it in side of glute.

  • Band above knees, not on kneecaps
  • Slow and controlled - don't use momentum
YouTube: E3 Rehab - Clamshells Done Right

Side-Lying Leg Raise

Key cues: Slight forward angle of leg (~10°). Lead with heel, not toe. Keep hips stacked.

  • Don't go too high - quality over range
  • Feel it in glute medius (side of hip)
YouTube: E3 Rehab - Side Lying Hip Abduction

Barbell Hip Thrust

Key cues: Shoulder blades on bench. Chin tucked (don't look at ceiling). Squeeze glutes at top, don't hyperextend back.

  • Drive through heels
  • Ribs down - don't flare
  • Pause 2 sec at top
YouTube: Bret Contreras - Hip Thrust Tutorial

Single-Leg Glute Bridge

Key cues: Keep hips level. Drive through heel. Squeeze glute at top.

  • Non-working leg can be straight up or bent
  • Don't let hips drop on one side
YouTube: E3 Rehab - Single Leg Glute Bridge

Cable Kickback / Hip Abduction

Key cues: Stand tall, don't lean. Keep working leg straight. Control the return.

  • Ankle strap above ankle bone
  • Feel it in glute, not lower back
YouTube: Mind Pump TV - Cable Kickbacks

Lateral Band Walks

Key cues: Stay low in mini squat. Keep tension on band always. Don't let knees cave.

  • Band above knees (easier) or around ankles (harder)
  • Take small, controlled steps
YouTube: E3 Rehab - Lateral Band Walks

Hip Adductor/Abductor Machine

Key cues: Sit tall, back against pad. Controlled movement both directions. Don't slam weights.

  • Adductor (squeeze in) = inner thigh
  • Abductor (push out) = glute medius/outer hip
YouTube: Renaissance Periodization - Hip Machines

Seated Calf Raise

Key cues: Full stretch at bottom (heel below platform). Pause at top. 3 sec eccentric (lowering).

  • Targets soleus (deep calf)
  • Ball of foot on edge, not toes
YouTube: Jeff Nippard - Calf Training Science

Standing Calf Raise (Smith)

Key cues: Knees straight (slight soft bend). Full range - stretch at bottom, squeeze at top.

  • Targets gastrocnemius (outer calf)
  • Don't bounce - control the movement
YouTube: Jeff Nippard - Standing Calf Raise

Eccentric Heel Drops

Key cues: Rise up on both feet, lower on ONE foot. 3 seconds down. Use other foot to come back up.

  • Based on Alfredson protocol for tendon health
  • Slight discomfort OK, sharp pain = stop
YouTube: E3 Rehab - Eccentric Heel Drops

Tibialis Raises

Key cues: Back against wall, feet out ~12 inches. Lift toes up toward shins. Slow and controlled.

  • Feel burn in front of shin
  • Balances calf work, prevents shin splints
YouTube: Knees Over Toes Guy - Tibialis Raise

Slant Board Calf Stretch

Key cues: Straight knee for gastroc, bent knee for soleus. Lean forward gently. Breathe into stretch.

  • Hold 30-60 sec per leg
  • No bouncing
YouTube: Squat University - Calf Stretching

Leg Press

Key cues: Feet shoulder width, middle of platform. Don't let lower back round at bottom. Control the descent.

  • Don't lock knees at top
  • Press through whole foot, not just toes
YouTube: Jeff Nippard - Leg Press Guide

Bulgarian Split Squat

Key cues: Rear foot elevated on bench/box. Torso upright. Front knee over ankle (not past toes). Back knee drops straight down.

  • Weight in front heel, not toes
  • Don't let front knee cave inward
  • More challenging than regular split squat - start light
YouTube: Squat University - Bulgarian Split Squat

Leg Extension

Key cues: Full range of motion. Squeeze quads at top. Control the lowering (2 sec).

  • Don't lock knees at top
  • Feel it in quads, not knees
  • Great for quad isolation
YouTube: Renaissance Periodization - Leg Extension

Walking Lunges

Key cues: Step forward into lunge. Drive through front heel to step forward. Keep torso upright.

  • Full stride length
  • Control the descent
  • Great for functional strength and balance
YouTube: Squat University - Walking Lunges

Single-Leg Leg Press

Key cues: One foot on platform. Control the descent. Press through whole foot. Focus on glute activation.

  • Start lighter than two-leg version
  • Great for addressing imbalances
  • Feel it in glutes and quads
YouTube: Single-Leg Leg Press

Prone Y-Raise

Key cues: Lie face down on bench. Arms in Y position (45° angle). Squeeze LOWER traps, not upper. Forehead down.

  • Very light weight (2 lbs) or bodyweight
  • Focus on lower trap squeeze, not upper trap
  • Key for neural re-education
YouTube: E3 Rehab - Prone Y T W

Frog Pumps

Key cues: Lie on back, soles of feet together, knees out. Drive through heels. Squeeze glutes at top.

  • Great glute activation exercise
  • Focus on glute squeeze, not lower back
YouTube: Frog Pumps

Goblet Squat

Key cues: Hold DB at chest, elbows down. Sit back and down. Knees track over toes.

  • Great for learning squat pattern
  • Go as deep as mobility allows with good form
YouTube: Squat University - Goblet Squat

Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

Key cues: Soft knees (slight bend), hinge at hips. Bar/DBs stay close to legs. Feel stretch in hamstrings.

  • Back stays flat - no rounding
  • Stop when you feel hamstring stretch, don't force depth
YouTube: Jeff Nippard - RDL Form

Single-Leg RDL

Key cues: Hinge forward as back leg goes back. Keep hips square. Light weight or bodyweight.

  • Touch wall or rack for balance if needed
  • Great for balance + hamstring
YouTube: Squat University - Single Leg RDL

Step-Ups (Forward & Lateral)

Key cues: Drive through heel of top foot. Don't push off back foot. Control the descent.

  • Box height: knee at 90° when foot is on box
  • Lateral: step to side, same cues
YouTube: Squat University - Step Ups

Prone Leg Curl

Key cues: Hips stay down on pad. Curl all the way up. Control the lowering.

  • Don't let hips lift off pad
  • Point toes to increase hamstring engagement
YouTube: Renaissance Periodization - Leg Curl

GHD Back Extension

Key cues: Hinge at hips, not lower back. Squeeze glutes at top. Don't hyperextend.

  • Pad at hip crease
  • Hands across chest or behind head
YouTube: Squat University - Back Extension

Bosu Balance / Squat

Key cues: Start with just standing on it. Progress to squats. Eyes forward, not down.

  • Dome side up is easier than flat side up
  • Great for ankle stability
YouTube: E3 Rehab - Bosu Ball Exercises

Dumbbell Bench Press

Key cues: Shoulder blades squeezed and DOWN. Feet flat on floor. Lower to chest level, press up.

  • Elbows at ~45° angle (not flared 90°)
  • Control the descent
YouTube: Jeff Nippard - DB Bench Press

Push-Up / Incline Push-Up

Key cues: Body in straight line. Elbows at 45°. Chest to floor (or close). Core tight.

  • Incline (hands elevated) = easier
  • Don't let hips sag or pike up
YouTube: Jeff Nippard - Perfect Push-Up

Lat Pulldown

Key cues: DEPRESS shoulders first (pull shoulder blades down), then pull bar to upper chest. Lean back slightly.

  • Don't shrug shoulders up
  • Feel it in lats (sides of back), not just arms
YouTube: Jeff Nippard - Lat Pulldown

Seated Cable Row

Key cues: RETRACT + DEPRESS shoulder blades. Pull to lower chest/upper abdomen. Squeeze 2 sec.

  • Don't lean back excessively
  • Feel squeeze between shoulder blades
YouTube: Jeff Nippard - Cable Row

Single-Arm DB Row

Key cues: Pull elbow back toward hip. Squeeze shoulder blade. Don't rotate torso.

  • Brace on bench with other hand
  • Row to hip, not to chest
YouTube: Jeff Nippard - Dumbbell Row

TRX Row

Key cues: Body straight like a plank. Pull chest to hands. Depress shoulders throughout.

  • Walk feet forward = harder
  • Great for scapular control
YouTube: TRX Training - TRX Row

Face Pulls → External Rotation

Key cues: Pull to face, then rotate hands outward. Squeeze lower traps. Elbows high.

  • Light weight, high reps
  • Great for posture and shoulder health
YouTube: Jeff Nippard - Face Pulls

Farmer Carry

Key cues: Shoulders DOWN (don't shrug). Stand tall, core braced. Walk with control.

  • Go heavy - grip should be challenged
  • Great for core, grip, and posture
YouTube: Squat University - Farmer Carry

Cable External Rotation

Key cues: Elbow pinned to side at 90°. Rotate forearm outward. Slow and controlled.

  • Light weight - this is for rotator cuff health
  • Keep shoulder down, don't shrug
YouTube: E3 Rehab - External Rotation

Scapular Push-Up

Key cues: Arms stay straight. Only move shoulder blades - squeeze together, then push apart (protract).

  • Great for serratus anterior
  • Small movement, big benefit
YouTube: E3 Rehab - Scapular Push-Up

Wall Slides

Key cues: Back and forearms flat on wall. Slide arms up and down. Keep contact with wall throughout.

  • Great for serratus and shoulder mobility
  • If hard to keep contact, stand further from wall

Chin Tuck (with Resistance)

Key cues: Make a "double chin." Push head straight back (not down). Hold against hand resistance.

  • Strengthens deep neck flexors
  • Key for forward head posture correction
YouTube: E3 Rehab - Chin Tucks

Levator Scapulae Stretch

Key cues: Tilt ear to shoulder → then rotate nose toward armpit. Gentle pressure with hand. Hold 30 sec.

  • Don't force - gentle stretch
  • Key for neck/shoulder junction pain
YouTube: E3 Rehab - Levator Scapulae Stretch

Foam Roll IT Band

Key cues: Side lying, roll from hip to just above knee. Slow - 1 inch per second. Pause on tender spots.

  • Support yourself with arms and top leg
  • Uncomfortable but shouldn't be excruciating
