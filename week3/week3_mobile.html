Exercise Tips

Tap to expand • Watch videos for form

Dead Bugs

▼

Key cues: Press lower back into floor. Move slowly. Exhale as you extend.

  • If back arches, reduce range of motion
  • Quality > speed
Watch Video

Bird Dogs

▼

Key cues: Keep hips level. Reach long, not high. Brace core.

  • Imagine balancing water on lower back
Watch Video

Side Plank

▼

Key cues: Stack shoulders over elbow. Hips up. Straight line head to heels.

  • Don't let hips sag
Watch Video

Clamshells

▼

Key cues: Keep feet together. Rotate from hip. Feel side of glute.

  • Band above knees
  • Slow and controlled
Watch Video

Chin Tuck

▼

Key cues: Make a double chin. Push head straight back. Hold against resistance.

  • Strengthens deep neck flexors
  • Key for forward head posture
Watch Video

Hip Thrust

▼

Key cues: Shoulder blades on bench. Chin tucked. Squeeze glutes at top.

  • Drive through heels
  • Don't hyperextend back
Watch Video

Lat Pulldown

▼

Key cues: DEPRESS shoulders first, then pull bar to chest.

  • Don't shrug shoulders up
  • Feel it in lats, not arms
Watch Video

Cable Row

▼

Key cues: RETRACT + DEPRESS shoulder blades. Pull to chest. Squeeze 2 sec.

  • Don't lean back too much
  • Feel squeeze between shoulder blades
Watch Video

Bulgarian Split Squat

▼

Key cues: Rear foot elevated on bench/box. Torso upright. Front knee over ankle. Back knee straight down.

  • Weight in front heel
  • Don't let knee cave in
  • More challenging than regular split squat - start light
Watch Video

Leg Extension

▼

Key cues: Full range of motion. Squeeze quads at top. Control the lowering (2 sec).

  • Don't lock knees at top
  • Feel it in quads, not knees
  • Great for quad isolation
Watch Video

Walking Lunges

▼

Key cues: Step forward into lunge. Drive through front heel to step forward. Keep torso upright.

  • Full stride length
  • Control the descent
  • Great for functional strength and balance
Watch Video

Single-Leg Leg Press

▼

Key cues: One foot on platform. Control the descent. Press through whole foot. Focus on glute activation.

  • Start lighter than two-leg version
  • Great for addressing imbalances
  • Feel it in glutes and quads
Watch Video

Prone Y-Raise

▼

Key cues: Lie face down on bench. Arms in Y position (45° angle). Squeeze LOWER traps, not upper. Forehead down.

  • Very light weight (2 lbs) or bodyweight
  • Focus on lower trap squeeze, not upper trap
  • Key for neural re-education
Watch Video

Frog Pumps

▼

Key cues: Lie on back, soles of feet together, knees out. Drive through heels. Squeeze glutes at top.

  • Great glute activation exercise
  • Focus on glute squeeze, not lower back
Watch Video

Eccentric Heel Drops

▼

Key cues: Rise on both feet, lower on ONE. 3 seconds down.

  • Alfredson protocol for tendon health
  • Slight discomfort OK, sharp pain = stop
Watch Video

Face Pulls

▼

Key cues: Pull to face, then rotate hands out. Squeeze lower traps.

  • Light weight, high reps
  • Great for posture
Watch Video

Romanian Deadlift

▼

Key cues: Soft knees, hinge at hips. Weight stays close to legs.

  • Back stays flat
  • Stop when you feel hamstring stretch
Watch Video
