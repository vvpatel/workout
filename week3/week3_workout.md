## TRAP SENSITIVITY PROTOCOL

**If upper traps feel tight/irritated (>5/10 discomfort):**

**Immediate (same day):**
- Stop all pulling exercises for that session
- Replace with: Cable Chest Fly (3 x 15) + Incline DB Press (2 x 12)
- Extra PT work: Prone Y-Raise (4 x 15), Levator Scap Stretch (2 min each side)

**Next 48 hours:**
- Heat + gentle stretching 2-3x daily
- Chin tucks: 3 x 10, every 2-3 hours
- NO pulling movements

**Next workout:**
- Reduce all pulling loads by 50%
- If still uncomfortable, skip pulling entirely for 1 week
- Focus on PT exercises only

**Return to normal when:**
- Trap tension is <3/10
- Can depress shoulders without discomfort
- No pain with cable rows at 50% load

**Lat Pulldown:** Slow progression from 20 → 40 lbs over 6 weeks (~3-4 lbs/week)

### Progression Rules

**For Leg Press, Leg Extension, Goblet Squat:**
- Add 10 lbs if you completed all reps with good form
- OR add 1-2 reps if weight felt heavy

**For Bulgarian Split Squat, Walking Lunges:**
- Try to add 1-2 reps first
- When you hit 10 reps, add 5 lbs and drop back to 8 reps
- Don't rush these - form matters more than weight

**For Leg Curl (Your Weak Point):**
- **This needs the most conservative approach.**
- Week 3: 20 lbs x 12
- Week 4: 25 lbs x 12 (only if Week 3 felt comfortable)
- Week 5: 30 lbs x 12 (only if no hamstring strain)
- Week 6: 35 lbs x 12
- Week 8: 40 lbs x 12
- Week 9: 45 lbs x 12

**Red flags - STOP progression if:**
- Hamstrings feel strained or painful (vs. good soreness)
- You can't complete all reps with full range of motion
- Form breaks down (jerking, using momentum)

**For RDL (Form-Dependent):**
- **DO NOT add weight unless form is perfect.**
- Progression checklist each week:
  - ☐ Bar stayed close to legs entire time
  - ☐ Felt stretch in hamstrings, not lower back
  - ☐ Back stayed flat, no rounding
  - ☐ Could have done 2 more reps with same form

**Only if all 4 are checked, add 10 lbs next week.**

If any are unchecked:
- Repeat same weight
- Film yourself
- Consider getting a form check from a trainer

**For Hip Thrust:**
- Add 10 lbs per week if form is good
- Focus on glute squeeze at top

## WEEK 7: DELOAD WEEK

**Purpose:** Allow tissue adaptation, prevent overtraining, assess progress

**Structure:**
- All weights at **60% of Week 6 loads**
- Same exercises, same reps, much lighter
- Focus on form refinement
- Keep cardio but reduce intensity (Zone 2 only, skip VO2 Max)

- Leg Press: If Week 6 was 105 lbs → Week 7 is 63 lbs (round to 65 lbs)
